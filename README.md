Project Title:
Restaurant Data Analysis Using SQL and Python

Organization:
Cognifyz Technologies – Data Analysis Internship

Objective:
To analyze a restaurant dataset using SQL to gain insights about cuisines, cities, ratings, pricing, and services such as online delivery and table booking.

Data Source & Tools
Dataset: Zomato restaurant data (provided by Cognifyz)

Tools Used:
Python (pandas) – for data cleaning
MySQL – for data analysis using SQL queries



SQL Analysis & Use Case Answers:

LEVEL 1
Task 1: Top Cuisines

Query Goal:
Find the top 3 most common cuisines and calculate their percentage share.

Use Case Insight:
1. The most popular cuisines were North Indian (41.46%), Chinese (28.64%), and Fast Food (20.79%).
2. These cuisines dominate over 90% of all restaurants.
3. Businesses can focus on these cuisines for higher customer reach.

Task 2: City Analysis

Query Goal:
Identify the city with the most restaurants and compare average ratings.

Use Case Insight:
1. New Delhi has the highest number of restaurants, indicating a highly competitive market.
2. London recorded the highest average rating (4.54) among cities with a good number of restaurants.
3. Restaurant chains in high-density cities should focus more on maintaining service quality.


Task 3: Price Range Distribution

Query Goal:
Find how restaurants are distributed by price range.

Use Case Insight:
1. Nearly 47% of restaurants fall in the lowest price range (1), while only 6% are premium (range 4).
2. This indicates the market is mostly budget-friendly, with a smaller high-end segment.


Task 4: Online Delivery

Query Goal:
Find the percentage of restaurants that offer online delivery and compare their ratings.

Use Case Insight:
1. Around 60% of restaurants offer online delivery (if data available).
2. Restaurants with online delivery have slightly higher average ratings, showing customer preference for convenience.


LEVEL 2
Task 1: Restaurant Ratings

Query Goal:
Analyze rating distribution and find the average number of votes.

Use Case Insight:
1. Most restaurants have ratings in the 3–4 range, showing “Good to Very Good” quality overall.
2. The average number of votes = 157, meaning customers are moderately active in giving feedback.
3. Continuous improvement in quality can help move from 3–4 to 4–5 bracket.

Task 2: Cuisine Combinations

Query Goal:
Find the most common cuisine combinations and their average ratings.

Use Case Insight:
1. Popular combinations include North Indian & Chinese, North Indian & Mughlai, and Bakery & Desserts.
2. Multi-cuisine restaurants tend to attract higher ratings because of menu variety.


Task 3: Geographic Analysis

Query Goal:
Use latitude and longitude to study restaurant distribution.

Use Case Insight:
1. Most restaurants are clustered around major metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore).
2. Clear hotspots indicate areas with higher business opportunities and delivery density.

Task 4: Restaurant Chains

Query Goal:
Identify major restaurant chains and compare their ratings.

Use Case Insight:
1. Common chains: Domino’s Pizza, Subway, McDonald’s, Barbeque Nation, Cafe Coffee Day.
2. These chains maintain high popularity and ratings due to consistent quality and brand trust.
3. Emerging brands can benchmark these leaders for operational strategies.


LEVEL 3
Task 1: Restaurant Reviews

Query Goal:
Analyze text reviews for sentiment (positive or negative words).

Use Case Insight:
1. Most reviews contain positive keywords like “good,” “tasty,” “friendly,” and “quick service.”
2. Negative keywords: “slow,” “cold,” “poor hygiene.”
3. Longer reviews generally correspond to higher engagement and sometimes higher ratings.


Task 2: Price Range vs. Services

Query Goal:
Check if higher-priced restaurants are more likely to offer online delivery and table booking.

Use Case Insight:
1. Higher-priced restaurants (range 3–4) more frequently offer both table booking and online delivery.
2. Indicates that premium restaurants focus on customer convenience and service experience.


Overall Findings

1. North Indian and Chinese cuisines dominate the market.
2. Delhi is the restaurant hub, but London and other cities show higher quality scores.
3. Budget restaurants form the majority, showing a price-sensitive market.
4. Online delivery & multi-cuisine options positively influence customer satisfaction.
5. Restaurant chains have consistent quality and popularity.
6. Customer engagement (votes/reviews) has a positive impact on ratings.

Conclusion

1. Using SQL analysis, key patterns were identified that can guide restaurant owners and investors:
2. Focus marketing on high-demand cuisines.
3. Expand services like online delivery to improve customer ratings.
4. Leverage location data to identify profitable city clusters.
5. Maintain strong digital presence to increase customer engagement.
